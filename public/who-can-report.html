Who Can Report & Who Can Be Reported

Anyone who has experienced a significant adverse event or a serious adverse event following a COVID modRNA injection administered under the direction or advice of a U.S. licensed physician and is within the statute of limitations (varying by state) can file a report.1 You may also file a report for a loved one who unfortunately has been incapacitated or has died following the gene based treatment.

Physicians who may be reported include:

  1. Private practice community physicians
  2. Employed health system physicians
  3. Chief medical officers (CMOs) of health systems where the injection was given
  4. CMOs of companies (e.g., pharmacies) where the injection was administered
  5. Medical directors of public health department where the injection was received
  6. Public health officials who recommended the injection

1 FDA — What is a serious adverse event?
